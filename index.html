Our technical progress

Elon Musk said in February 2014 that the Colonial Transporter Martian would be "100 times larger than an SUV" and able to take 100 people to Mars at a time.

The engine fuel Raptor is almost entirely burnt methane and liquid oxygen, based on the previous techniques used by SpaceX for their rocket systems, planned for missions to Mars. This means that the Raptor engine can spew about 306'175 kilograms of thrust, and is about three times more powerful than the Merlin engines of the Falcon 9.

Due to the presence of water in the Martian subsoil and carbon dioxide in the atmosphere of the Mars planet, methane, which is a simple hidrocarbon, can be easily synthesized on the red planet using the Sabatier reaction.
